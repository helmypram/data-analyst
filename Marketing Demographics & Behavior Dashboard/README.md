Digital Ad Campaign Targeting & Optimization Analysis

Description: This project focuses on analyzing user behavior and demographics to optimize digital advertising campaigns. By evaluating historical data on user engagement (time spent on site, daily internet usage) and demographic profiles (age, area income), the project identifies key characteristics that distinguish users who click on ads from those who ignore them. The primary objective is to help the marketing team minimize wasted ad spend, combat "banner blindness," and increase the overall Click-Through Rate (CTR) by targeting the most high-converting user segments.

Tools: Tableau Public, Google Slides

Skills: Data validation, feature engineering (extracting temporal features like Hour, Day, and Month from Timestamps), demographic profiling, Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), correlation analysis, and business analytics.

Outputs:

Interactive Tableau Dashboard: Visual analysis of user behavior patterns (Time on Site vs. Age), income distribution, and hourly click-through rates (CTR) in relation to ad conversion status.

Business Presentation (Google Slides): A strategic summary outlining the ideal target audience profile (e.g., focusing on older demographics with lower internet usage times) and actionable recommendations to optimize ad scheduling and targeting parameters.

Datasets:

advertising.csv - Demographic profile and browsing behavior of website visitors.

Fields: 13 (10 original features + 3 engineered temporal features)

Records: 1,000

Acknowledgements: The dataset is publicly available on Kaggle and widely used for marketing analytics and classification tasks - Advertising Dataset. (https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/gabrielsantello/advertisement-click-on-ad?select=advertising.csv)
